How this works

To hit an aggressive target size, each page is rendered to an image and re-saved as a compressed JPEG inside a new PDF. This is the only way browser-side compression can reliably reach large reductions (e.g. 355MB → 5MB). The tradeoff: text becomes part of an image and is no longer selectable or searchable. The tool automatically searches across JPEG quality and resolution to get as close to your target as possible without going over.
